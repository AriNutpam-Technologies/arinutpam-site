What it sees

Six inspections.
One pass of the cone.

Three cameras capture the tip, tail, front and rear under both white and UV light. A specialised AI model scores each view independently.

T1 · CAM1
◎

Tip Pattern ID

Classifies the wound tip across up to 18 pattern types, flagging the wrong colour or winding scheme.

T2 · CAM1
▣

Tail Inspection

Binary OK/NG grading of the cone tail for malformed or defective windings.

T3 · CAM2
✂

Cut-End Detection

Spots loose, cut or protruding ends on the front face before they reach packing.

T3 · CAM2
✿

Flower-Nose Detection

Identifies the flower-nose forming defect on the front of the package.

T5 · CAM3
▦

Surface Defects

Front and rear surface grading for soiling, damage and winding irregularities.

T4·T6 · UV
☀

UV Inspection

Dedicated UV-light models surface contamination and oil marks invisible under white light.

From trigger to verdict

A closed loop with your line

Yarn Inspector drops into your existing PLC and conveyor. No cloud, no internet exposure — it runs on-prem on your production network.

PLC Trigger

The line signals a cone in position over Modbus TCP, with its carrier ID.

MODBUS · T1–T6

Multi-View Capture

Three cameras snap tip, tail, front and rear under white and UV light.

3 CAM · 6 VIEWS

GPU Inference

TensorRT-optimised YOLOv8 models score each view on an NVIDIA Triton server.

YOLOv8 · TRITON

PLC Write-Back

Pass/fail per view is written back to the carrier register — the line accepts or rejects.

RESULT · <5s

Log & Analyse

Every result is stored, charted and reportable — per cone, per shift.
